Rheumatic heart disease

What is it?

Rheumatic heart disease is a condition in which the heart valves have been permanently damaged by rheumatic fever. The heart valve damage may start shortly after untreated or under-treated streptococcal infection such as strep throat or scarlet fever. An immune response causes an inflammatory condition in the body which can result in on-going valve damage.

Causes of it

Rheumatic heart disease is caused by rheumatic fever, an inflammatory disease that can affect many connective tissues, especially in the heart, joints, skin, or brain. Usually occurs in children ages 5 to 15

Symptoms of it

  • Fever
  • Shortness of breath
  • Weakness
  • Chest pain
  • Swelling
  • Uncontrolled movements of arms,legs or facial muscles

How to lower the risk

Rheumatic heart disease can be prevented by preventing strep infections or treating them with antibiotics when they do occur.

Treatment for it

Treatment depends in large part on how much damage has been done to the heart valves. In severe cases, treatment may include surgery to replace or repair a badly damaged valve. The best treatment is to prevent rheumatic fever. Antibiotics can usually treat strep infections and keep rheumatic fever from developing. Anti-inflammatory drugs may be used to reduce inflammation and lower the risk of heart damage.
