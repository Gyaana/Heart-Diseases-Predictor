Pulmonary Embolism

What is it?

A pulmonary embolism is a blood clot that blocks and stops blood flow to an artery in the lung. In most cases, the blood clot starts in a deep vein in the leg and travels to the lung. Rarely, the clot forms in a vein in another part of the body.

Causes of it

Pulmonary embolism is caused by a blocked artery in the lungs.Your risk for PE is high if you have deep vein thrombosis (DVT) or a history of DVT. In DVT, blood clots form in the deep veins of the bodyâ€”most often in the legs.

Symptoms of it

  • Shortness of breath.
  • Chest pain.
  • Fainting.
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Lightheadedness or dizziness
  • Excessive sweating
  • Fever

How to lower the risk

  • Stopping smoking
  • getting regular exercise.
  • Not sitting still for a long time
  • Avoid becoming dehydrated.
  • Maintaining a healthy weight, by eating a healthy balanced diet.

Treatment for it

  • Treatment is aimed at keeping the blood clot from getting bigger and preventing new clots from forming. Immediate treatment is essential to prevent serious complications or death.
  • Blood thinners or anticoagulants (Anticoagulants are medicines that help prevent blood clots.) are the most common treatment for a blood clot in the lung.
  • Medication and surgery are two mediums for treatment
